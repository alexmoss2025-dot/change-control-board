Change Control Board: Stakeholder Responsibility Mapping

Comprehensive Role Definitions, RACI Matrix & Recommended Assignments
📅 April 15, 2026 📄 Version 1.0 📋 Document ID: CCB-SRM-001 🔒 Confidential — Internal Use Only

1. Executive Summary

This document provides a comprehensive mapping of all stakeholder roles, responsibilities, and recommended personnel for the Moss Energy Change Control Board (CCB). It was compiled by analyzing 13 project artifacts including workflow flowcharts, swimlane diagrams, meeting minutes, transcripts, templates, the ITIL 4 Foundation reference, and the CCB UC-Demo presentation.

The CCB is a centralized governance body established to standardize change management across all Moss Energy technology platforms. The initiative follows the ADKAR Model (Awareness → Desire → Knowledge → Ability → Reinforcement) and is guided by ITIL 4 Change Enablement best practices. Oracle Fusion has been designated as the pilot use case, with planned rollout to all other technology teams upon successful validation.

This mapping identifies 10 distinct roles across 7 process stages and 7 quality gates, with real personnel recommendations drawn from meeting participants and organizational discussions. The approval structure has been consolidated to two primary approvers — the Business Product Owner and the Technical/System Product Owner — streamlining from the original four-approver model.

2. CCB Roles Overview

The Change Control Board operates with five core roles (as defined in the CCB UC-Demo presentation) plus supplemental roles identified through workflow analysis and meeting discussions.

3. Detailed Role Descriptions

👤

Role 1: Change Owner

CORE

The initiator and end-to-end owner of the change request. Typically a Project Manager, initiative lead, or an IT analyst who identifies that a support request requires a change.

Key Responsibilities

  • Initiate the Change Request in Clarity
  • Complete Section 1 (Change Identification) and Section 2 (Change Category)
  • Provide business justification and link related ServiceNow tickets
  • Coordinate with Technical Lead for technical sections
  • Complete Section 8 (Post-Implementation Review Plan)
  • Close the CR after successful implementation and PIR

Process Stage Involvement

  • Stage 1: Initial Submission (Primary)
  • Stage 5: Implementation Execution (Co-owner)
  • Stage 6: Post-Implementation Review (Primary)
  • Stage 7: Closure & Archive (Primary)
Recommended Personnel: Varies per change request. The analyst working on the support case who determines a change is needed becomes the Change Owner. For project-driven changes, this would be the assigned PM or initiative lead.
Example: For Oracle Fusion — the Finance IS analyst assigned to the ticket; For Procore — the requesting engineer (e.g., Rob Waki per discussion).
🔧

Role 2: Technical Lead

CORE

The implementation owner who ensures the technical feasibility, completeness, and stability of the proposed change. Owns the production deployment runbook and rollback procedures.

Key Responsibilities

  • Complete Section 3 (Technical Specifications)
  • Complete Section 4 (Risk Assessment Summary)
  • Complete Section 5 (Implementation Schedule)
  • Complete Section 6 (Rollback & Recovery Plan)
  • Complete Section 7 (QA & Testing Evidence)
  • Execute or oversee the implementation in production
  • Coordinate with the Implementer/Assigned Analyst

Process Stage Involvement

  • Stage 2: Technical Development (Primary — Sec 3–7)
  • Stage 3: Rework if completeness review fails
  • Stage 5: Implementation Execution (Primary)
  • Stage 6: PIR sign-off
Recommended Personnel (by platform):
  • Oracle Fusion: Jeff or Sheena (Navneet's Finance IS team members)
  • Procore: Andrew (reviews changes, coordinates with business, delegates to analysts)
  • Project Fusion (future): Frank (not yet in production)
Note: Per the April 10 discussion, the Technical Lead is the person who coordinates the change and presents at the CCB meeting. They may delegate actual implementation to a lower-level analyst but retain oversight and accountability.
💼

Role 3: Business Product Owner

CORE

The module-level business owner who approves what is changing. Confirms that UAT has been completed, testing evidence is satisfactory, and training documentation is up to date.

Key Responsibilities

  • Review and understand the proposed change
  • Confirm UAT/testing has been performed
  • Verify training documentation is current
  • Provide formal business approval (Section 7 & Section 9)
  • Determine who in the business should be communicated
  • Not required to attend CCB meetings if approval is captured pre-meeting

Process Stage Involvement

  • Stage 1: Identified in CR submission
  • Stage 2: Validates UAT evidence
  • Stage 4: Approval captured (pre-meeting or during CCB)
  • Stage 7: Sign-off on closure

Role 4: Technical / System Product Owner

CORE

The system-level owner who approves how the change is implemented in production. Reviews the implementation runbook, rollback procedures, and assesses upstream/downstream impacts to maintain production stability.

Key Responsibilities

  • Review deployment/implementation runbook
  • Verify rollback procedures are adequate
  • Assess upstream and downstream system impacts
  • Approve the technical implementation plan (Section 9)
  • Ensure production stability is not compromised
  • May serve as CCB Voting Member for their platform

Process Stage Involvement

  • Stage 2: Review technical specifications
  • Stage 4: Provide approval during CCB meeting
  • Stage 5: Oversee implementation execution
  • Stage 6: Participate in PIR

Role 5: CCB Lead / Facilitator

CORE

Coordinates the entire CCB process from intake to closure. Validates quality gates, facilitates CCB meetings, manages the approval workflow, and approves the Post-Implementation Review.

Key Responsibilities

  • Validate Gate 1 (Sections 1–2 completeness)
  • Jointly review Sections 1–7 with Product Owner (Gate 2.5)
  • Schedule and facilitate CCB meetings
  • Manage approval workflow and notifications
  • Reclassify changes if needed (Standard → Normal → Major)
  • Approve PIR at Gate 5
  • Manage escalations (SLA breaches, deadlocks)
  • Maintain the Change Request Register

Process Stage Involvement

  • Stage 1: Gate 1 validation
  • Stage 3: Completeness Review (with Product Owner)
  • Stage 4: Facilitate CCB meeting
  • Stage 6: Gate 5 — Approve PIR
  • Stage 7: Oversee closure and archival
  • All Stages: SLA monitoring and escalation
Confirmed Personnel: Alex Diamant — Self-identified as CCB Lead during the April 10 follow-up meeting: "The CCB lead, which is me. I basically am going to be coordinating the entire CCB process." Also serves as Note Taker for CCB meetings and co-presenter of the CCB UC-Demo.
🗳️

Role 6: CCB Board / Voting Members

GOVERNANCE

Cross-functional stakeholders including platform owners, technical leads, and business representatives who review change requests and vote on approval. Board composition may vary by change request based on subject matter relevance.

Key Responsibilities

  • Review CR packages distributed before CCB meetings
  • Attend CCB meetings (biweekly cadence planned)
  • Assess risk, impact, and readiness of proposed changes
  • Vote: Approve / Approve with Conditions / Defer / Reject
  • Executive approval (Gate 2) for Critical risk changes
  • Gate 3 (CCB Verdict) and Gate 4 (Implementation authorization)

Process Stage Involvement

  • Stage 2: Gate 2 — Executive approval if Critical
  • Stage 4: CCB Decision & Gate 3
  • Stage 5: Gate 4 — Implementation authorization

Role 7: Approval Groups

GOVERNANCE

Specialized review groups that provide domain-specific sign-off during the CCB meeting. These replace the former separate pre-CCB approval collection stage.

Approval Group Domains

  • IT Security: Security vulnerabilities, access control, data protection
  • Infrastructure: Capacity, network, environment, deployment requirements
  • Business Owner: Business justification, cost-benefit, stakeholder impact
  • Compliance / Regulatory: Regulatory requirements, audit trail, documentation

Process Stage Involvement

  • Stage 4: Review and sign off during CCB meeting
  • SLA: 3 business days for pre-review of CR package
Recommended Personnel: To be designated by each functional area head. Approval group leads should be identified from existing IT Security, Infrastructure Operations, Business Leadership, and Compliance teams.
👔

Role 8: Executive Sponsors (CTO / CIO)

ESCALATION

Senior executives required to approve Major changes (CTO + CIO) and Emergency changes (CTO). Also serve as escalation point for approval deadlocks and SLA breaches.

Key Responsibilities

  • Approve Major changes (CTO + CIO sign-off required)
  • Authorize Emergency changes (expedited verbal/email approval)
  • Resolve approval deadlocks escalated by CCB Lead
  • Receive emergency change override notifications
  • Ensure post-facto CCB review for emergency changes within 5 business days

Process Stage Involvement

  • Stage 4: Approval for Major/Emergency changes
  • Escalation: Deadlock resolution, SLA breaches
Recommended Personnel:
  • Strategic Direction: Ravi (provides direction for the CCB initiative per Victor Velasco)
  • Executive Visibility: JD (visibility and oversight per meeting discussions)
  • Leadership Communications: Cheryl Willis (communicates CCB initiative to leadership)
Note: Specific CTO and CIO names were not explicitly named in the materials. These positions should be filled by the current Moss Energy CTO and CIO.
🛠️

Role 9: Implementer / Assigned Analyst

EXECUTION

The person who performs the actual technical implementation in production. May be the Technical Lead or a delegate. Per principle, only personnel supporting production stability should touch the production environment.

Key Responsibilities

  • Execute the implementation runbook step-by-step
  • Follow the rollback plan if issues arise
  • Coordinate with Technical Lead during deployment
  • Report implementation status and any deviations
  • Captured in a dedicated "Assigned To" field in the CR form

Process Stage Involvement

  • Stage 5: Implementation Execution (Primary executor)
Recommended Personnel: Varies per change. For Oracle Fusion, this would be a Finance IS team member (e.g., Jeff or Sheena) working under Navneet's oversight. For Procore, lower-level analysts delegated by Andrew. For net-new applications, may be an SI consultant who then hands off the deployment runbook.
💻

Role 10: Clarity Tool Administrator

TOOLING

Maintains the Clarity platform as the central system of record for all change requests. Manages form configurations, notification workflows, SharePoint integration, and user access.

Key Responsibilities

  • Configure and maintain the Clarity intake form
  • Build notification workflows for stage transitions
  • Manage SharePoint integration for document uploads
  • Set up Business Product Owner registry as dropdown values
  • Grant user access (e.g., production access for Navneet)
  • Implement linked/dependent drop-down lists

Process Stage Involvement

  • All Stages: System infrastructure & automation
  • Automated email notifications (34 total across 7 stages)
Confirmed Personnel: Zorin Wade — Built the Clarity intake form, demonstrated the approval process, manages SharePoint integration. Described by Alex Diamant as "very effective in helping with the Clarity portion." Also co-presenter of the CCB UC-Demo.

7. Recommendations for Role Assignments

7.1 Immediate Actions (Pre-Launch)

  1. Formalize the Business Product Owner Registry: Document Business Product Owners by module for Oracle Fusion (assigned to Navneet/Alex, per April 3 Action Item #5). Ravi's Procore mapping should serve as the template.
  2. Clarify "Technical Lead" Terminology: Per feedback from Arun Gannarapu, Monica Palacios, and Navneet Kaur (April 10), the term "Technical Lead" causes confusion between project-phase development leads and production support leads. Consider renaming to "Production Technical Lead" or "Implementation Coordinator" to distinguish from project-phase roles.
  3. Add "Assigned To / Implementer" Field: Capture the actual person performing the implementation, separate from the Technical Lead who reviews and approves (identified in April 10 discussion).
  4. Grant Clarity Access: Ensure all identified personnel have appropriate access levels in Clarity (Action Item #1 from April 3 — grant Navneet production access).

7.2 Process Refinements

  1. Pilot with Oracle Fusion: Oracle Fusion is confirmed as the pilot use case. Navneet Kaur's team will validate the process, after which it rolls out to all other technology teams.
  2. Define Maintenance & Release Windows: Currently not standardized. Establish frequency, timing, and duration for deployment windows across platforms.
  3. Communication Plan for Changes: Per Monica Palacios (April 10), include a field in the CR form to capture who in the business needs to be communicated about the change.
  4. Establish CMDB: Until a Configuration Management Database is established as the source of record, Business Product Owner assignments will be maintained manually. Plan for CMDB implementation as a medium-term goal.

7.3 Governance Cadence

  • CCB Meetings: Biweekly (with potential shift to monthly once stabilized)
  • First CCB Review Session: Targeted for June 4, 2026
  • Change Intake in Clarity: Begins May 1, 2026
  • Pre-reads: Distributed 48 hours before each CCB meeting
  • Quorum: Minimum number of voting members required (to be defined)
